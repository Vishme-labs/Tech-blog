Quantum-Safe Cryptography: Preparing for the Quantum Era

Published: November 28, 2025

The Quantum Threat

Large quantum computers could break widely used public-key cryptosystems like RSA and ECC. Organizations with long-lived confidentiality needs must begin planning migration to quantum-resistant algorithms now to avoid future data exposure.

Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

PQC algorithms, standardized by bodies such as NIST, aim to replace vulnerable primitives with schemes based on lattice problems, hash-based signatures, and code-based cryptography that resist quantum attacks.

Migration Strategies

Adopt hybrid crypto that combines classical and PQC primitives to mitigate risk during transition. Inventory cryptographic assets, prioritize systems with long confidentiality windows, and plan for key sizes and performance implications.

Operational Considerations

Implement secure libraries, test interoperability, and monitor standards updates. Consider hardware constraints, bandwidth impacts, and backward compatibility when deploying PQC schemes.
