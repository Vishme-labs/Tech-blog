Foundation Models: Large Pretrained Models Explained

Published: November 28, 2025

Defining Foundation Models

Foundation models are large-scale pretrained models trained on broad data that can be adapted to many downstream tasks. By pretraining on diverse corpora, they capture general-purpose representations that reduce data needs for task-specific fine-tuning.

Capabilities and Risks

These models power translation, summarization, multimodal tasks, and more. However, they also present risks including biases in training data, high computational cost, and potential misuse—requiring careful governance and evaluation.

Fine-Tuning and Prompting

Practitioners leverage fine-tuning, prompt engineering, and adapters to specialize foundation models for specific use-cases, balancing performance, cost, and safety considerations.

Responsible Deployment

Responsible deployment involves evaluation for fairness, continuous monitoring, and access controls. Open research and shared benchmarks support better understanding and safer use of foundation models.
